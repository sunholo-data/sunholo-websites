The Studio

A quiet
practice

Founded by master potter Kenji Tanaka, our studio blends centuries-old Raku techniques with modern design. Each piece is unique — shaped by intention, altered by fire, finished by silence.

We work with the philosophy of wabi-sabi: finding beauty in imperfection, transience, and incompleteness. Our tableware is made to be used, to age gracefully, and to carry the quiet dignity of natural materials.

Every bowl, cup, and vessel that leaves our studio has passed through the hands of its maker. There are no two alike. That is not a limitation — it is the whole point.

Workshop Programme

Learn the art of
making

All classes are held in our studio in Kyoto. Small groups only.

01

Wheel Throwing

Beginner · 2 hours

An introduction to the potter's wheel. Learn to centre clay, raise walls, and shape your first vessel under gentle guidance. No experience needed.

02

Raku Firing

All levels · 4 hours

Experience the ancient Japanese Raku firing process — fast-fire kilns, smoke reduction, and the unpredictable beauty of flame-kissed surfaces.

03

Kintsugi Repair

All levels · 3 hours

The art of repairing broken ceramics with gold. Learn how to transform damage into beauty, honouring a piece's history rather than hiding it.
