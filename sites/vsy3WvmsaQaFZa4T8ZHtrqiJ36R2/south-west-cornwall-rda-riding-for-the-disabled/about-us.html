About & Policies

Our history, our commitment, and our standards of care.

Our Story

SW Cornwall Group was formed in 1976 to provide horse-riding for disabled children from Nancealverne Special School. Over the years, we have expanded to accommodate adults and riders with varied disabilities, including autism. Based at Old Mill Stables, we continue a legacy of nearly five decades, fostering a bond between horses and humans that transcends physical and cognitive barriers.

Trustee Perspective

"I started volunteering 3 years ago as I was about to retire. Watching the enjoyment on the children’s faces and seeing them progress both physically and behaviorally makes it a worthwhile commitment."
— Anthea Carr, Group Secretary & Trustee

Safety & Policies

We maintain the highest standards of safety for our participants and volunteers. Our 2025 Venue Risk Assessment is available for review, covering everything from mounting facilities to emergency procedures.

Our commitment to safety is absolute. Every session is supervised by qualified RDA coaches, and all equipment is regularly inspected to meet the rigorous standards of the national Riding for the Disabled Association.
