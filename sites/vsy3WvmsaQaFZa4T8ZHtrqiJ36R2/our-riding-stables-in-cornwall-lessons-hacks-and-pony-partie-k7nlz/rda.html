About the RDA

What is the Riding for the Disabled Association?

The Riding for the Disabled Association (RDA) is a national charity that enables people with disabilities to benefit from the physical and mental health benefits of horse riding and carriage driving.

Founded in 1969, the RDA has grown into a network of over 500 groups across the UK, supporting more than 25,000 disabled children and adults each week. Every group is run by trained coaches and supported by dedicated volunteers.

Therapeutic riding offers benefits that go far beyond the saddle — improving balance, coordination, muscle strength and posture, while also building confidence, communication skills and emotional wellbeing.

The benefits of therapeutic riding

  • Improves balance, posture and core strength
  • Develops coordination and motor skills
  • Boosts confidence and self-esteem
  • Encourages communication and social skills
  • Reduces anxiety and promotes emotional wellbeing
  • Provides a sense of freedom and independence
  • Creates meaningful connections — with horses and with people
  • Simply brings enormous joy and fun
“The horse moves in a rhythm that mirrors the human gait — for many disabled riders, this is the closest they will come to the feeling of walking.” — RDA therapeutic riding principle

Our group

RDA South West Cornwall Group

Old Mill Stables in Lelant Downs is the home of the Riding for the Disabled South West Cornwall Group — a dedicated team of coaches, volunteers and wonderful horses and ponies working together to make a difference.

Our group serves disabled riders of all ages from across the South West Cornwall area, including St Ives, Penzance and the wider West Penwith peninsula. We welcome new riders throughout the year, subject to assessment and availability.

We are passionate about ensuring that every person who comes through our gates — whether a rider, a visitor or a care home resident on a Tea with a Pony visit — leaves feeling valued, supported and uplifted.

Our sessions are held in our indoor arena, which means we can ride whatever the Cornish weather brings!

The human-horse bond

More than just riding

At Old Mill Stables, we believe that the bond between a person and a horse is something truly special — something that cannot be replicated. Whether it is a young rider taking their first steps in the saddle, or an elderly visitor sharing a quiet moment with a pony, the connection is profound and deeply human.

For many of our riders and visitors, the relationships they build — with our horses, with our volunteers, and with each other — are among the most meaningful in their lives.

This is why we do what we do. And why we will never stop.

Get involved

Volunteer with us

Our group could not exist without our wonderful volunteers. Whether you love horses or simply want to make a difference, there is a role for you at Old Mill Stables.

🚶

Side-walker

Walk alongside riders during sessions, providing physical support, encouragement and safety. No horse experience needed.

🐎

Horse Leader

Lead the horse during sessions, working closely with the rider and side-walkers. Horse experience is helpful but training is provided.

🎪

Events & Fundraising

Help organise and run our events — cream teas, pony parties, Christmas celebrations and more. Great fun and great company.

🏥

Outreach Support

Help with our community outreach visits — including Tea with a Pony sessions for care homes and supported living groups.

All volunteers receive full training and support. You do not need equestrian experience to make a huge difference to our riders' lives. Just bring enthusiasm and a big heart.

Help us keep riding

As a charitable RDA group, we rely on fundraising, donations and community support to keep our sessions running. Every contribution — whether attending a cream tea, volunteering your time, or making a donation — makes a direct difference to the disabled riders and communities we serve.

Our annual fundraising events are a wonderful part of community life in West Cornwall. We are always grateful for support of any kind.

If you are a local business or organisation and would like to support our work through sponsorship or partnership, please do get in touch.
