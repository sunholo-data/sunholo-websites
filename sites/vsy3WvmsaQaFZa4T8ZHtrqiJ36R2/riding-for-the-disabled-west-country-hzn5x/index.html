About the RDA

Riding for the Disabled Association

The Riding for the Disabled Association (RDA) helps people with disabilities and health conditions benefit from the power of horses. Across the UK, local RDA groups provide riding, carriage driving, and horse-care activities tailored to each individual's needs and abilities.

Our West Country group — Southwest RDA (SWCG) — is based at Old Mill Stables. We welcome riders of all abilities and backgrounds. Whether you are returning to riding after illness or injury, or picking up the reins for the very first time, we are here to help.

Riding builds confidence, strength, and mobility. But more than that — it creates friendships, routine, and a quiet kind of joy that is very hard to find anywhere else.

A Member's Story

Nine Years with Max — and How It Started

I wasn't always disabled but became disabled in childhood. You begin to understand the world is full of things you can't do. Can't complete or continue education, can't get a steady job, can't have a social life, can't drive, maybe can't live independently.

One of the many things I couldn't do was go horse riding. It was something I enjoyed up until I couldn't do it any more. I was maybe 14 when I had to stop. The horse I learned on was called Jake. A round barrel, naughty hedgerow nibbler.

"I had never heard of the Riding for the Disabled Association. Probably five days and one form later I was speaking to a member of my local RDA at Old Mill Stables."

At some point I was at a summer barbecue with friends and was talking about how I loved the pictures she'd put on her socials of her rides. That I missed it. She asked why I wasn't riding with the RDA.

They had a space for me and I was soon mounting the horse I was to relearn to ride on. Jake. Another round barrel boy who liked a treat and would pretend he needed the bathroom if he didn't want to do something. But with him I learned what I could do, having never thought I'd ride again — and with some trial, error and one minor fall I was ungracefully mounting and dismounting my trusty steed unaided.

My confidence leapt as I trained to compete. I was moved onto Max. A wilful lad who mostly does whatever he likes. He's clever though, and after a battle of wills we've been together for six of the nearly nine years I've been with Southwest RDA.

"Riding has helped my mobility and makes me feel strong and capable. Max is not my horse but he is a grounding and a consistency."

Even the times I have been unable to ride I am still learning horsecare while I groom him, enjoying a quiet time just being in each other's company. He likes apples and oat bars.

I have just turned 40 and while I can't continue my education, have steady employment or drive a car — I have Max, and the RDA made that possible.

What We Offer

Riding Is for Everyone

The RDA welcomes people of all ages and disabilities. Here is what you can expect when you get in touch.

🐎

Adapted Riding

Sessions designed around your individual needs, with trained volunteers supporting you every step of the way.

🌿

Horse Care

Learn grooming, feeding, and stable management. Spending time with horses is therapeutic even when not in the saddle.

🏆

Competition

When you are ready, train to compete. RDA competitions are supportive, celebratory events for all abilities.

🤝

Community

A warm, welcoming group of riders, volunteers, and staff who understand your journey.
